Role of Senator

Until submission of the constitution for this year this list may be altered

Expectations of Senators
  • Keep in contact with Department specific organizations
  • Host at least 1 event every semester
  • Keep in constant contact with department advisers
  • Respond to all VGSA emails and notices in a timely manner
  • Attend at least 1 GSG meeting per semester
  • Participate in a VGSA committee
  • Attend weekly meeting, unless other arrangements made with Executive VP
  • Be available to students to address concerns
  • Attend at least 50% of all VGSA events
  • Be the Single Point-of-Contact for students of the respective department for non-academic affairs. Any issue with an academic intent or background should be first discussed with the department's Graduate Student Advisor(s)
  • Attend weekly council meetings, unless other arrangements are made with Executive Vice President
  • Assist with the VGSA activities and post VGSA flyers in the department they specifically represent
  • Forward all official VGSA e-mails to department advisors so they can forward it to all students in their graduate program
First semester at USC?

That's ok! We understand that there will be a learning curve and we can work with you. We are looking for open minded, enthusiastic, committed students for senator positions.

As a senator how much time would I be expected to commit?
How much time you spend per week on this responsibility is really up to the student. However on a weekly basis as a bare minimum average it would be about 2 hours per week.
If you are interested in run for a senator position:

Email vgsapres@gmail.com with your full name as recognized by USC and your department. Once the application becomes available it will be forwarded.

What might I expect on the application?

The application will not be long, most questions will be short. At the end there will be 2 short answer questions. We will also request a pdf of your class schedule and resume

Even if you choose not to run as a senator please be sure to vote if there is an election in your department! Details for voting should be available the second week of school.
